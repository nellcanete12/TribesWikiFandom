Description

Minerals are extracted from the earth and form the foundation of mid-game progression. They unlock access to Iron Nuggets and Steel, which are essential for crafting advanced weapons and equipment. Minerals also enable the production of Gold Dust used by the Taga-Ilog tribe. Without a reliable mineral supply, your settlement will struggle to advance beyond early game.

How to Get Minerals

Build and upgrade the Miner's Tent to produce minerals. Assign workers to increase production rate.

Minerals are produced by the Miner's Tent building. To maximize mineral production:

1. Build Miner's Tents — Minerals are typically unlocked after establishing food and wood production
2. Upgrade them for better yields — Higher level mining buildings produce more minerals per tick
3. Assign workers strategically — Up to 30 workers can be assigned to Miner's Tents. Each worker produces at a rate of 0.3125 minerals per tick
4. Plan for mid-game needs — Minerals are the gateway to advanced crafting and tribe-specific progression

Usage

Minerals are used to unlock advanced crafting and mid-game progression:

⚒️ Iron & Steel Production — Minerals are refined into Iron Nuggets and Steel for weapons
💎 Gold Dust Creation — Enables Taga-Ilog tribe-specific crafting recipes
🗡️ Advanced Weapons — Crafting higher-tier weapons requires mineral-based materials
🛡️ Equipment Upgrades — Enhancing equipment often requires mineral components
🏛️ Late-Game Research — Advanced upgrades and technologies require minerals

Mid-Game Bottleneck

⚠️ Important: Minerals are a mid-game bottleneck without proper mining infrastructure. Once you unlock the Miner's Tent, prioritize its construction and worker assignment. Many players overlook mineral production early, leading to delays in advancing to steel and advanced equipment.
