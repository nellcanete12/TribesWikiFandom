Description

Wood is essential for construction and crafting in Tribes of Malaya. It enters almost every recipe in the game, making it a critical resource for progression. Whether you're building new structures, upgrading existing ones, or crafting weapons and equipment, wood is always in demand. A stable wood supply is crucial to preventing bottlenecks in your settlement's development.

How to Get Wood

Build and upgrade the Woodcutter's Tent to produce wood. Assign workers to increase production rate.

Wood is produced by the Woodcutter's Tent building. To maximize wood production:

1. Build multiple Woodcutter's Tents — More buildings = more total production
2. Upgrade them strategically — Higher level buildings produce more wood per tick
3. Assign workers optimally — Up to 30 workers can be assigned to Woodcutter's Tents. Each worker produces at a rate of 0.3125 wood per tick
4. Plan for recipes — Wood is used in almost every crafting recipe, so ensure production meets your crafting demands

Usage

Wood is one of the most versatile resources in Tribes of Malaya:

🏗️ Building Construction — Most buildings require wood to construct
⚒️ Crafting Recipes — Nearly every crafted item requires wood as an ingredient
🛠️ Upgrades — Upgrading buildings and equipment requires wood
🎯 Equipment Production — Crafting weapons and armor consumes wood
🛡️ Defenses & Structures — Building fortifications and structures requires lumber

Early Game Priority

💡 Pro Tip: Wood enters almost every recipe in the game. Get the Woodcutter's Tent running early so you never bottleneck on lumber for crafting or building. A steady wood supply is essential for early game progression.
